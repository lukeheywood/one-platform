The System Atlas is the canonical, public map of ONE.

It documents **what has stabilised**, how it is structured, and where authority and responsibility sit.
It is intentionally limited in scope: the Atlas describes reality as it exists today, not everything that may exist in the future.

This is the entry point for understanding ONE.

---

## How to use the Atlas

- Start with **System Authority** to understand ownership and accountability.
- Review **Orientation** to understand the fixed semantic constraints the system operates under.
- Use **Capabilities** to see what exists today versus what is explicitly out of scope.
- Refer to **Architecture** for how components relate at a high level.
- Follow **Governance** links for contracts, invariants, and promotion rules.
- Inspect **Case Studies** for concrete, evidence-backed examples under load.

The Atlas links outward to specialist repositories and artifacts.
It does not duplicate implementation details.

## Scope boundary

The Atlas intentionally excludes:
- runtime code
- private data
- internal logs
- speculative designs
- unfinished experiments

Only stabilised, inspectable material is promoted here.
